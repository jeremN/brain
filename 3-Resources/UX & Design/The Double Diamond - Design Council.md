# The Double Diamond - Design Council

**Source:** [https://www.designcouncil.org.uk/our-resources/the-double-diamond/](https://www.designcouncil.org.uk/our-resources/the-double-diamond/)
**Category:** UX & Design

## Summary

The official Design Council page on the Double Diamond framework, the universally accepted visual representation of the design process. Created in 2003 by the Design Council to codify and popularize the design process. The four phases: Discover (understand the real problem through research), Define (use insights to reframe the challenge), Develop (explore different solutions through co-design), and Deliver (test solutions at small scale, iterate). Later evolved into the Framework for Innovation to account for iteration and the non-linear nature of real design work.

## Why It's Interesting

The canonical reference for the most widely-used design process framework — essential foundation for any product/UX work

## Key Takeaways

- Four phases: Discover (research & understand), Define (reframe the challenge), Develop (co-design solutions), Deliver (test & iterate)
- First diamond is about finding the right problem — 'understand, rather than simply assume, what the problem is'
- Second diamond is about finding the right solution — diverge with different answers, then converge on what works
- Evolved into Framework for Innovation: acknowledges that insights can send you back to discovery, and no idea is ever 'finished'
